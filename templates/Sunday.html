What will you do this weekend? Sunday

Our Sunday recomendations


Now for some Sunday is more about recovery, a time to chill out and rest your sore head. But for others it's another day to carry on the party.So here are some suggestions for whatever takes your fancy, from a Sunday roast feast fit for a king to great places to catch up with old friends, we've got you covered.

ENJOY:

The Crown and Two Chairmen

A great pub to go to on a Sunday, this is an amazing spot for celeb spotting as well! Plus they're dog friendly - unheard in central London.

When to go: Wanting enjoy Soho but with mans best friend.

 Check out their website

The Green

Hailed as one of the top places for a Sunday roast by TimeOut, this modern gastropub is a must. It boasts a gorgeous airy dining room and even better food.

When to go: For the best Sunday roast

Check out their website

Freedom Bar

An absolute must if you're in the Soho area. Great for a catch up with friends, and plus it closes pretty late if you want to keep the party going!

When to go: If you want to keep partying all the way into Sunday

Check out their website

The Dove

In the mood to indulge in some history? The Dove has been around since the 17th century. Although truth be told, most visitors come for its lovely riverside terrace overlooking the houseboats.

When to go: To enjoy history and a great view with a drink in hand

Check out their website

Hot Box

Now no Sunday list is complete without the best place for a bloody mary, as its the ultimate in hangover cure. Hot Box's cocktail bar boasts a few variations of the classic for those feeling bold enough.

When to go: For one of the best Bloody Marys in London

Check out their website

The Ship

Now if you've not heard of this South London gem shame on you! The Ship boasts an epic outdoor area overlooking the Thames, making it the ultimate in lazy summer drinking.

When to go: Chilled group drinks in the sunshine

Check out their website
